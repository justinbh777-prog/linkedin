## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A 34-unit Club Pilates operator just got bought by private equity.

They didn't stop there. They scooped up a 6-unit and a 3-unit franchisee in the same move.

Here's the interesting part.

Buyers in fitness are getting pickier. They're paying close attention to your management team, your growth rights, and how easily your business runs without you.

The gap between an "A" business and a "C" business is getting wider.

What does that mean if you own 1 to 20 fitness units?

The number you sell for has less to do with size and more to do with quality. A clean, well-run 3-unit operation can trade at a strong multiple. A messy 10-unit one might not.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I see this all the time.

Buyers pay up for businesses that don't depend on the owner.

If you're the one opening the doors and running every shift, that's a discount waiting to happen.

Three things to watch:

- Whether your business can run without you
- Your room to grow (open new units or buy nearby operators)
- How clean your books and systems look to a buyer

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** Why some franchise brands are much easier to sell than others

---

Two franchises. Same revenue. One sells in 60 days. The other sits for a year.

I see this all the time.

The difference usually has nothing to do with how much money the business makes.

It comes down to the brand behind it.

Buyers do their homework now. They look at how the franchisor is doing. They check unit growth. They read the franchise disclosure document. They talk to other owners.

If the brand is opening new locations and owners are happy, buyers line up. Financing gets easier too. Lenders trust brands with a strong track record.

But if the brand is shrinking, or owners are quietly unhappy, buyers get nervous. Even a great-looking location becomes a hard sell.

I had two owners last year with nearly identical numbers. One was with a growing brand and closed fast. The other was tied to a struggling franchisor and took months, with a lower price.

Here is the takeaway. Your exit value is tied to your brand's health, not just yours.

Watch how your franchisor is doing. It matters more than most owners think.
