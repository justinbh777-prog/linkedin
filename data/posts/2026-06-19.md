## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A 34-unit Club Pilates operator just got bought by private equity.

And they didn't stop there. They scooped up a 6-unit and a 3-unit operator in the same move.

Here's what caught my eye.

The buyer said something important. The gap between A, B, and C assets is widening.

Translation: clean, well-run businesses still command top dollar. Messy ones are getting marked down.

Buyers are also paying for "white space" - room to grow. More territory. More units they can add.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, this is the quiet shift small owners miss.

Buyers aren't just buying your revenue. They're buying how easy you are to take over.

A strong manager who can run the place without you? That moves you from a B asset to an A asset. Same revenue. Higher price.

I've seen two owners with identical numbers sell for very different multiples. The difference was almost always the team and the systems.

Three things to watch:

- Whether your business runs without you in it
- Open territory the buyer can grow into
- Clean books that survive a recast

## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two franchise owners. Same brand. Same unit count. One sells for way more than the other.

Here is why.

A private equity firm just bought into Club Pilates. They picked up a 34-unit operator, plus a 6-unit and a 3-unit shop.

What they paid was not the same across the board.

Buyers are pulling apart the "A" deals from the "B" and "C" deals. The gap between them is growing.

What separates them? Stability. Room to grow. And a management team that runs without the owner in the building.

For a 1 to 10 unit owner, this is the whole game.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, the biggest value-killer I see is owner dependence.

If the business stops when you leave, buyers see risk. Risk means a lower price.

The owners who get top dollar built a team and a system that runs without them. That is what makes you an "A" deal.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run for two weeks without you?
- Do you have open territory left to grow?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** The difference between owners who get top dollar and those who don't

---

The owners who get top dollar usually decide to sell three years before they sell.

That gap is everything.

I've closed over 100 franchise deals. The pattern is always the same.

The owners who win aren't the ones with the best location or the lowest costs. They're the ones who got their business ready while they still had time.

Clean books. Documented systems. A team that runs the place without them in the building every day.

The owners who lose money wait until they're tired or burned out. Then they call me wanting to sell next month.

I had one owner who ran two strong units. Great revenue. But everything lived in his head. No manager. No clean records. Buyers got nervous, and the offers came in low.

A buyer isn't paying for how hard you work. They're paying for what keeps working after you leave.

So if you think you might sell someday, start acting like it now. Build the business so it doesn't need you.

That's what gets top dollar.
