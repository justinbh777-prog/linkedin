# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** Why building a business that runs without you is the best exit strategy

---

The buyers don't want you. They want your business.

I've closed over 100 franchise deals, and this is the thing owners get wrong.

They spend 20 years becoming the person who holds everything together. Then they go to sell, and the buyer sees a problem.

If the owner leaves, does the business leave with him?

That fear shows up in the offer. Lower price. More money tied to you staying around. Longer transition periods you don't want.

I worked with an owner last year who ran four units. Great revenue. But he was the only one who knew the vendors, the schedule, the hiring. Everything ran through his phone.

We had to delay the sale a full year just to build a team that could run it without him.

Compare that to owners who built real systems. Managers who handle the day to day. Those businesses sell faster and for more.

The best exit strategy isn't a sales tactic. It's making yourself unnecessary long before you sell.

Start now, even if your exit is five years out.
