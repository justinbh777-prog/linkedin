## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A 34-unit Club Pilates operator just got a private equity buyer.

Eagle Merchant Partners bought a majority stake in that group last year. They added a 6-unit and a 3-unit operator too.

Why fitness? They liked that Pilates has been around 100 years and is still growing.

Here's the catch. Buyers are getting pickier.

There's now a real gap between "A" assets and "C" assets. Same brand, very different price.

What separates them? A strong management team. Room to grow. A business that runs without the owner glued to it.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I see this all the time.

Two owners with the same unit count get very different offers.

The difference is rarely the brand. It's whether the business can stand on its own.

Buyers pay up for stability. They discount for risk.

Three things to watch:

- Does your business run without you in the building daily?
- Do you have white space to add units or grow same-store sales?
- Is your team strong enough to survive a transfer?

## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two fitness owners with the same unit count just got two very different offers.

One walked away with a premium. The other got a discount.

The difference? Buyers stopped paying the same price for every deal.

Eagle Merchant Partners just reentered fitness by backing a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. Smart money is flowing in again.

But here is what changed.

Buyers are now sorting deals into A, B, and C piles. Your management team, your growth rights, and how clean your operation runs decide which pile you land in.

For a 1-20 unit owner, this is huge.

A few years ago, a warm market lifted everyone. Now buyers are picky.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I see this play out constantly.

The owner who built a team that runs without them gets the premium.

The owner who IS the business gets marked down. Buyers see risk, not value.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you for 30 days?
- Do you still have open territory to grow into?
- Are your numbers clean and easy to verify?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What I tell every franchise owner who calls me in a panic to sell fast

---

"Sell it fast" is the most expensive sentence in this business.

When an owner calls me in a panic, the first thing I do is slow them down.

Panic and speed cost money. Every time.

A rushed sale tells the buyer something is wrong. They smell it. Then they lower their offer, add conditions, and drag their feet anyway.

I had an owner call me ready to dump three units because he was burned out. He wanted out in 30 days.

We took 90 instead. Cleaned up his books. Fixed a few staffing gaps. Got his numbers to tell the real story.

The final price was 40 percent higher than what he would have taken in that first phone call.

Same business. Same owner. Just a little patience.

Here is what I tell everyone. The thing pushing you to sell fast is usually emotional. The thing that gets you paid is preparation.

If you are tired, that is normal. But tired is not a strategy.

Slow down first. Then sell.
