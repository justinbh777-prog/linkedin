# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What SBA lenders look for that most sellers never think about

---

Most franchise deals don't fall apart over price.

They fall apart over the SBA loan.

Here's what almost no seller thinks about. When a buyer needs financing, the lender isn't really betting on the buyer. They're betting on your business.

And they look at things you've probably ignored for years.

Clean books. Do your tax returns match your P&L? If they don't, that's a red flag.

Add-backs. Sellers love to run personal expenses through the business. The car. The phone. The "consulting." Every dollar of that makes your cash flow look smaller to a lender.

Customer concentration. If one account is half your revenue, the lender gets nervous.

I had a deal last year where the business was strong, the buyer was qualified, and the price was fair. But the books were messy and the add-backs weren't documented. The loan took an extra four months. We almost lost the buyer.

The takeaway is simple. Get your financials clean two years before you sell. Not two months.

The lender approves the loan. But your paperwork approves the lender.
