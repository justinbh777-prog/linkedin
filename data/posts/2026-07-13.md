# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What 100+ franchise transactions taught me about what buyers really want

---

Buyers don't buy your business. They buy the version of it that runs without you.

After 100+ franchise deals, that's the biggest thing I've learned.

Owners think buyers want high revenue. Sure, that matters. But it's not what makes them nervous.

What makes a buyer walk is a business that only works because the owner is there every day.

I once had a seller with great numbers. Strong location. Loyal customers. But he ran everything himself. No manager. No systems. Just him.

Buyers loved the business. Then they realized they were really buying his job. Offers came in low.

Compare that to a smaller owner I worked with. Less revenue. But he had a solid team and clear processes. He could step away for weeks.

That business sold faster and for a better multiple.

The lesson is simple. Buyers pay for freedom. If your business depends on you, you're the asset they can't buy.

Start building a business that runs without you long before you sell.
