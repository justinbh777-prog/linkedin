# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What I wish every franchise owner knew about SBA financing

---

Most franchise deals don't die because of price.

They die because of SBA financing.

Here's what I wish more owners understood.

When a buyer uses an SBA loan to buy your business, the bank isn't just looking at them. They're looking at your books too.

Sloppy records slow everything down. Mixed personal and business expenses. Missing tax returns. Cash that never hit a deposit slip.

The bank sees all of it.

I had a deal last year where the buyer was approved, the price was agreed, and everyone was ready. Then the lender asked for three years of clean financials.

The owner couldn't produce them. The deal took an extra four months and almost fell apart.

Not because the business was bad. Because the paperwork wasn't ready.

Here's the takeaway.

If you want a smooth sale, clean up your books two to three years before you sell. Keep business and personal separate. Report your real income.

The buyer's financing depends on your numbers looking solid.
