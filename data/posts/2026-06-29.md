## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two fitness operators sell for very different multiples. Same brand. Same revenue.

What's the difference? Buyers stopped paying the same price for every deal.

A private equity firm just bought into a 34-unit Club Pilates operator, plus a six-unit and a three-unit franchisee.

But here's what changed. Buyers are now digging hard into three things: how the business runs without you, room to grow, and the strength of your team.

For the 1-20 unit owner, this matters more than the headline.

A few years ago, an "A" deal and a "C" deal sold close together. Not anymore. The gap is widening.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you what separates an A from a C.

It's rarely the revenue. It's whether the business depends on the owner.

If you're the one opening, closing, and fixing every problem, you're a C. Build a team that runs it, and you jump tiers.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you for 30 days?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What I tell every franchise owner who calls me in a panic to sell fast

---

The fastest way to lose money on your franchise? Sell it in a panic.

I get these calls a few times a month. An owner is stressed, tired, or facing something personal. They want out, and they want out now.

Here is what I tell every one of them.

Buyers can smell desperation. The second they know you have to sell fast, your price drops. Sometimes by a lot.

I had an owner call me ready to take the first offer that came in. We slowed down for 60 days. Cleaned up the books. Fixed two small issues that scared buyers off. The final sale price was 30 percent higher.

Sixty days. Thirty percent.

The lesson is simple. The best time to sell is before you feel like you have to. A little planning protects the value you spent years building.

If you are feeling that pressure right now, take a breath first. The rushed deal is almost never the best deal.
