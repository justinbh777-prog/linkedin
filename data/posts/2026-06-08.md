## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A 34-unit Club Pilates operator just got bought by private equity.

And they didn't stop there. They scooped up a 6-unit and a 3-unit operator in the same move.

This is how consolidation works in fitness right now.

Here's the part that matters for you.

Buyers are getting pickier. They're sorting deals into A, B, and C piles.

A and B businesses are still trading strong. The C pile is where prices fall apart.

What separates them? Stability, room to grow, and a management team that runs without the owner.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I'll tell you the management team is the one most owners ignore.

If your business stops working when you go on vacation, you're a C asset. No matter how good your numbers look.

The owners who build a team that runs the show get paid more. Every time.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you in the building?
- Do you have white space to add units, or are you maxed out?
- Are your last three years of numbers clean and easy to verify?

If you own a franchise and have ever thought about an exit, even 3-5 years out, now is the time to understand your number. DM me.

## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two franchisees of the same brand can sell for completely different numbers.

That gap is getting wider, not smaller.

A private equity firm just bought into a 34-unit Club Pilates operator, plus a 6-unit and a 3-unit alongside it.

Why those? Stability, room to grow, and a real management team.

Buyers used to pay close to the same for an A operator and a C operator. Not anymore.

Now they dig into how the business runs, whether you have growth rights left, and who keeps things going when you step away.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I see this firsthand.

The owners who get top dollar are not always the ones with the most units. They are the ones whose business runs without them.

A 5-unit operator with clean books and a strong manager often beats a 10-unit owner who is the business.

Three things to watch:

- Whether your business can run without you in the building
- How much room you have left to open new units

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What I tell every franchise owner who calls me in a panic to sell fast

---

"Sell it fast" is the most expensive sentence in this business.

When someone calls me in a panic, it's never really about the business.

It's a health scare. A divorce. A partner who wants out. Something pushed them to the edge, and now they want to be done yesterday.

I get it. But a rushed sale is a discounted sale. Buyers can smell desperation, and they price it in.

Last year an owner called me wanting to close in 30 days. He had two profitable units but messy books and no manager in place. Buyers saw the chaos and lowballed him hard.

We slowed down. Cleaned up the financials. Trained a manager so the business could run without him.

Eight months later he sold for almost 40 percent more than the first offers.

Here's what I tell every panicked caller: the urgency you feel is rarely the urgency the deal needs.

A few months of prep can mean a much bigger check.

Selling under pressure almost always costs you. Selling with a plan almost always pays.

If you are thinking about an exit - even years from now - let's talk.
