They also scooped up a 6-unit and a 3-unit operator in the same deal.

The buyer said something most owners miss. The gap between an "A" business and a "C" business is getting wider.

Same brand. Same category. Very different price.

What separates them? Stability. Room to grow. And a management team that runs the place without the owner in the building every day.

For a 1-20 unit owner, this is the whole ballgame.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you buyers are pickier than they were two years ago.

They pay top dollar for clean books and a business that does not depend on you.

The good news? Most of these gaps are fixable with 2-3 years of lead time.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you for 30 days?

Buyers used to pay similar prices for good and great businesses. Not anymore.

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** The conversation every franchise owner should have 3 years before selling

---

Most owners start planning their exit way too late.

By the time they call me, they want to sell in 6 months. That is the worst time to start.

The best conversation happens 3 years out. Not because you are ready to leave. Because 3 years gives you time to fix the things buyers actually pay for.

Here is what I mean.

Buyers look at clean books. They look at whether the business runs without you. They look at steady revenue, not one lucky year.

Most of that cannot be fixed in 6 months. But 3 years? That is enough time to change everything.

I worked with an owner once who was too involved in daily operations. Great business, but he was the business. We spent two years building a team and clear systems. When he finally sold, he got a much higher price than he would have the year we met.

The takeaway is simple.

Your exit is not an event. It is a process. And the earliest work pays the most.
