# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** A mistake franchise owners make when thinking about selling

---

Most franchise owners wait too long to sell.

Not because they don't want to exit. Because they think they need "one more good year" first.

I hear it all the time. "Let me just hit my numbers this year, then I'll sell for more."

Here is what usually happens instead.

That extra year brings a rent increase. Or a new competitor. Or the owner burns out and the business slips. Suddenly the value they were chasing is gone.

I worked with an owner last year who ran three units. Strong locations, loyal staff, clean books. He was ready. Then he decided to wait for "a better market."

Six months later a key manager quit and sales dropped 15 percent. The offer he could have taken was no longer on the table.

The lesson is simple. The best time to sell is when the business is healthy and you still have energy to run it well.

Buyers pay for momentum, not potential.

If you are waiting for the perfect moment, you might be waiting past your peak.
