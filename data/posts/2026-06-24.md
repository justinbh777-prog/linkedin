## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two fitness operators can have the same revenue and sell for wildly different prices.

Here's why.

A private equity firm just reentered fitness by buying a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. Plus a six-unit and a three-unit franchisee.

But buyers aren't paying the same for everyone anymore.

The gap between "A" businesses and "B" businesses is widening. Same revenue. Different value.

What makes an "A"? Stability. Room to grow. And a management team that runs without the owner.

For a 1-20 unit owner, this is the whole ballgame.

If your business stops when you step away, you own a job, not an asset.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I see this kill deals constantly.

Owners think revenue sets their price. It doesn't. Transferability does.

The owner who built a team that runs the day-to-day gets the premium. The one doing everything himself takes a discount, or no deal at all.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run for 30 days without you?
- Do you have open territory left to grow into?
- Is your revenue recurring or one-time?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What buyers pay a premium for that has nothing to do with revenue

---

Two businesses with the same revenue. One sold for double the other.

The difference had nothing to do with the numbers.

Buyers pay a premium for businesses that can run without the owner.

That sounds simple. But most owners are the business. They hold the customer relationships. They train every new hire. They solve every problem that walks in the door.

When a buyer sees that, they get nervous. Because if you leave, the value walks out with you.

I worked with two owners last year doing nearly identical revenue. One had a strong general manager and clean systems. The other answered every call himself.

The first owner got multiple offers and a premium price. The second struggled to find a buyer at all.

Same revenue. Very different outcomes.

Here is the takeaway. If you want a strong exit, start making yourself less important now. Build the team. Write down the systems. Step back where you can.

You cannot do this in the 90 days before a sale. It takes time.
