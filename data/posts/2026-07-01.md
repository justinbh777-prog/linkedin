# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** The conversation every franchise owner should have 3 years before selling

---

Most owners start planning their exit two months before they want out.

That's about three years too late.

Here's what I've learned after closing 100+ deals. The best sale price doesn't come from the listing. It comes from what you do in the years before you list.

Buyers pay for clean books. They pay for a business that runs without you. They pay for numbers that trend up, not sideways.

None of that happens overnight.

I worked with an owner who wanted to sell fast. His numbers were fine, but he was the business. Every decision ran through him. Buyers saw that and got nervous.

We paused. Spent 18 months building a manager who could run the place. When he finally sold, he got a much stronger price and a smoother close.

So here's the takeaway.

Start the conversation early. Not because you're selling tomorrow, but because the moves you make now decide what you walk away with later.

Three years of small changes beats three months of scrambling.
