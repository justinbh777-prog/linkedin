## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A private equity firm just backed a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. Plus a 6-unit and a 3-unit deal on the side.

Here is what caught my eye.

Buyers are getting picky. The gap between an A-quality business and a B-quality one is growing.

But here is the good news. A and B assets are still trading at strong levels. It is the C assets getting punished.

What separates them? Stability. Room to grow. And a management team that runs the business without the owner in the building.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, this is the lesson I repeat most.

Buyers do not just pay for revenue. They pay for a business that keeps running when you leave.

If every decision flows through you, you own a job, not an asset. That is what drops you from an A to a C.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business grow, or are you out of open territory?
- Does your team run daily operations without you?
- Are your numbers clean and easy to verify?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** Why the best time to think about your exit is right now

---

The best time to sell is before you have to.

I've closed over 100 franchise deals in 20 years. The owners who get the most money almost never sell in a rush.

Here's what I see too often.

An owner waits until they're burned out. Or their health changes. Or the market turns. Then they call me wanting to sell fast.

Fast selling costs money. Every time.

I worked with an owner last year who called me two years before he actually wanted to exit. We spent that time cleaning up his books and fixing a few operational gaps.

When he finally sold, he got 30% more than his first valuation. That extra time paid for itself many times over.

Thinking about your exit does not mean you're leaving tomorrow. It means you're building a business that's ready when you are.

The owners who plan early always have more options. And more leverage.

You don't need a timeline. You just need to start the conversation.
