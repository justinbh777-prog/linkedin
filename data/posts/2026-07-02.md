# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What SBA lenders look for that most sellers never think about

---

Most deals don't die over price. They die in the SBA underwriting.

I've watched clean-looking sales fall apart because the seller never understood what the lender actually cares about.

Here's the part most owners miss.

SBA lenders aren't just buying your revenue. They're buying how transferable your business is without you in it.

If you're the reason customers come back, that's a red flag. If half your sales run through your personal cell phone, that's a problem. If your books mix personal and business spending, the loan slows down or stalls.

I had a seller with strong numbers and a motivated buyer. The deal dragged for months because his financials didn't cleanly separate owner perks from real profit. The lender couldn't see the true cash flow, so they hesitated.

The takeaway is simple.

Start thinking like a lender two years before you sell. Clean books. Documented systems. A business that runs without you touching everything.

That's what gets deals funded fast.

Most sellers focus on the buyer. The smart ones prepare for the bank.
