## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A private equity firm just bought three Club Pilates operators at once. One had 34 units. The others had six and three.

Here's the interesting part.

Buyers aren't paying the same for every deal anymore. They're separating the A businesses from the B and C ones.

The gap between them is growing.

What earns the top price? Stability, room to grow, and a management team that runs without the owner.

For a 1-20 unit owner, this is the whole game. If your business only works because you show up every day, you're a C asset. And C assets get discounted.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you this is the number one thing owners underestimate.

Buyers aren't just buying your revenue. They're buying whether the business runs without you.

The owner who spent two years building a manager who could take over? That owner got paid more. Every time.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run for 30 days without you?
- Do you have clear white space to add units or grow?
- Are your numbers clean and easy to verify?

## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two fitness owners with the same unit count can sell for wildly different numbers.

That gap is getting wider.

Buyers just poured money into a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. But they passed on plenty of others.

Why? They now split businesses into A, B, and C grades. And the C grade is getting crushed.

The A and B businesses still trade at strong prices. The weak ones sit.

Here is what separates them: a real management team, room to grow, and a business that runs without the owner glued to it.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, this is the shift I keep seeing.

A few years ago, almost anything sold. Not anymore.

Buyers dig in now. They ask who runs the business when you are gone. They ask where the next unit comes from.

If the answer is "me" and "nowhere," your multiple drops.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you for 30 days?
- Do you have open territory or growth rights left?
- Is your team strong enough to keep going after a sale?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What I tell every franchise owner who calls me in a panic to sell fast

---

"Sell it fast" is usually the worst thing you can do.

When someone calls me in a panic, they want out yesterday. A health scare. A burnout. A bad month that felt like the end.

I get it. But panic is expensive.

The buyers who move fast are the ones who smell desperation. They know a rushed seller will take less. Much less.

Here is what I tell every one of them.

The difference between a fast sale and a smart sale is usually 90 days. Not two years. Ninety days.

I had an owner ready to dump his three units for almost nothing because he was exhausted. We spent a few weeks cleaning up his books and getting the story straight.

He closed for nearly double his first "just get me out" number.

Nothing about the business changed. Only the preparation did.

So if you are feeling that urge to sell fast, take a breath first. The panic is real, but the deadline in your head usually is not.

A little patience is worth a lot of money.
