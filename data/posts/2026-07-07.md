## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A private equity firm just bought a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. Plus a 6-unit and a 3-unit franchisee in the same deal.

Here is what caught my eye.

The buyer did not chase the biggest name. They chased stability and room to grow.

Pilates has been around 100 years. It is a workout people add on top of other workouts, not one they quit. That kind of staying power drives value.

But there was a catch. Buyers are getting pickier. The gap between an A business and a C business is wider than it used to be.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, this is the shift I keep seeing.

A strong management team is no longer a nice-to-have. It is the difference between a full-price offer and a discount.

Buyers want a business that runs without you in the room. If everything depends on the owner, that is a red flag, not a selling point.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you day to day?
- Is there white space to add units nearby?
- Are your numbers clean and easy to verify?

## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two fitness businesses can look identical on paper and sell for wildly different prices.

Same units. Same revenue. Different buyer interest.

The gap? Buyers now separate A, B, and C assets more than ever.

An investor just backed a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. What made it an "A" deal wasn't size. It was room to grow and a management team that runs without the owner.

Here is what that means for you.

If your business only works because you show up every day, buyers see risk. They pay less.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you the "A" sellers all have one thing in common.

They built a business, not a job.

They have territory left to grow into. They have people who can run the day-to-day. That is what closes the price gap.

The good news? You can fix most of this before you ever sell.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run for two weeks without you?
- Do you have open territory or room for more units?
- Is your revenue recurring or one-time?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** The real reason most franchise deals fall apart

---

Most franchise deals don't die over price.

They die over trust.

After 100+ closed transactions, I've learned the number one deal killer isn't the money. It's what surfaces once the money is agreed on.

Sloppy books. Missing lease documents. A franchisor approval nobody planned for. A seller who "forgot" to mention two underperforming units.

None of that kills a deal on day one. It kills the deal on day 40, when the buyer starts wondering what else they weren't told.

I watched a solid five-unit deal fall apart last year. Good price. Motivated buyer. But the owner couldn't produce clean numbers, and every delay chipped away at the buyer's confidence. By the time the paperwork was ready, the buyer had walked.

Here's the takeaway.

Buyers aren't just buying your cash flow. They're buying certainty. The cleaner and more honest your business looks, the less room there is for doubt to creep in.

Get your house in order long before you list. It protects your price and your timeline.
