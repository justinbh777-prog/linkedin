# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** The conversation every franchise owner should have 3 years before selling

---

Most owners call me when they want to sell. That's already two years too late.

Here's what I've learned after 100+ deals.

The best exits aren't decisions. They're setups. And the setup starts about three years out.

That's when you should be having one honest conversation with yourself: what is a buyer actually paying for?

Because they're not buying your hard work. They're buying your numbers, your systems, and how well the business runs without you.

I worked with an owner last year who had four strong units. Great revenue. But he was the business. He ran everything. Buyers saw risk, not value, and the offers reflected it.

Compare that to an owner who spent three years building a manager layer and clean books. Same size. Far cleaner exit. Much higher price.

The difference wasn't luck. It was lead time.

So if selling is anywhere on your mind, even loosely, ask yourself now: would this business be worth buying if I stepped away tomorrow?

The answer tells you exactly what to fix.
