Buyers today are pickier than they were two years ago. They separate the A, B and C operators and pay accordingly.

What does that mean if you own a few units?

The gap between a "good" business and a "great" one is widening. Same sales, different value.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you the premium is in the things you can build now.

A team that runs without you. Open territory to expand. Steady, repeat members.

Those three things move your number more than one strong year ever will.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run without you in the building daily?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** A mistake franchise owners make when thinking about selling

---

Most franchise owners wait too long to sell.

They wait for the "perfect" year. Record revenue. A flawless P&L. The moment when everything finally lines up.

That moment rarely comes.

Here's what I see after 20+ years in this business. Buyers don't just pay for your best year. They pay for what your business can do next.

I worked with an owner who held on for one more "big" year. He wanted to sell at the top. But sales softened. A key manager left. His best location lost its lease.

Two years later, his business was worth less, and he was more burned out than ever.

The truth is simple. The best time to sell is usually when things are going well and you still have energy left to run the place.

Not when you're exhausted. Not when the numbers are sliding.

Selling from a position of strength gets you more money and more options. Selling from a position of stress gets you neither.

If you're even thinking about an exit, start the conversation early. You don't have to act today.
