# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What I tell every franchise owner who calls me in a panic to sell fast

---

"Sell it fast" is the most expensive sentence in this business.

When someone calls me in a panic, I slow them down.

A rushed sale is a discount sale. Buyers smell urgency, and they price it in.

I had an owner call me last year. Burned out. Wanted out in 30 days. Ready to take almost anything.

We cleaned up his books first. Sorted out one bad lease. Showed three years of real numbers instead of a messy pile.

It took four extra months. He sold for almost 40 percent more than his first rushed offer.

Here is what I tell everyone.

The thing pushing you to sell fast is rarely the business. It is stress, a partner issue, or fear. Those feel like emergencies. They usually are not.

The business is an asset. Selling it well takes a little time and a clear head.

If you are in panic mode right now, breathe. Then make a plan instead of a fire sale.

You built something. Do not give it away because one bad week made you want to run.

If you are thinking about an exit - even years from now - let's talk.
