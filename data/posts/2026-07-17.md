# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** What buyers actually look for that most sellers never prepare

---

Most sellers think buyers care about revenue.

They don't. Not first, anyway.

After 100+ deals, I can tell you what buyers actually look at before anything else: how much the business depends on you.

If you are the reason the location runs, that scares buyers. It means when you leave, the profit might leave too.

They want to see systems. A manager who can run the day-to-day. Staff who stay. Numbers that are clean and easy to follow.

Had an owner last year with strong sales but he did everything himself. Opened the store. Handled payroll. Knew every customer by name. Buyers loved him and passed on the deal. The business was really just him.

Compare that to a smaller operator with lower sales but a solid manager and clean books. Multiple offers. Sold fast.

Here is the takeaway. Start working on the business, not just in it, long before you plan to sell.

The best time to prepare is two to three years out. The worst time is the day you decide you are done.
