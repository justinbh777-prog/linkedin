## VARIANT A — Financial Hook

A private equity firm just bought into a 34-unit Club Pilates operator. Plus a 6-unit and a 3-unit franchisee alongside it.

That tells you something.

Buyers are getting pickier. They are sorting franchise businesses into A, B, and C buckets.

The gap between an A deal and a C deal is wider than it used to be.

But here is the good news. A and B assets are still trading at strong, stable numbers.

For the 1-20 unit owner, this is what matters. Your valuation depends on how "clean" your business looks to a buyer.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you the difference between an A and a C deal is rarely the revenue.

It is the management team. Can the business run without you?

It is the growth story. Is there white space left to build?

Get those two things right and you move up a bucket. That bucket is worth real money.

Three things to watch:

- Whether your business can run without you in it
- Open territory left for a buyer to grow into
- Recurring revenue that holds steady month to month

## VARIANT B — Contrarian Hook

Two fitness businesses can post the same revenue and sell for wildly different prices.

Buyers stopped paying flat multiples. They now sort deals into A, B, and C.

The gap between them is getting wider.

A 34-unit Club Pilates operator just took on a private equity partner. Why? Stable model, room to grow, and a strong team running it.

That last one matters most.

Here is what this means for you. If your business only works because you show up every day, buyers see risk. That drops you from an A to a C fast.

As someone who works with franchise buyers and sellers every day, I can tell you the owners who get top dollar all have one thing in common.

They built a team that runs the business without them.

Stability and open territory help. But a manager who can operate without you is what turns a good multiple into a great one.

Three things to watch:

- Can your business run for two weeks without you? Buyers will ask.
- Do you have room to grow, either by adding units or acquiring nearby ones?
- Is your revenue steady month to month, or does it swing?

# POST 2 — Personal Post

**Topic:** The most common thing that kills a franchise deal at the last minute

---

Most deals don't die over price.

They die over messy books.

I've watched it happen too many times. Buyer is excited. Terms are agreed. Everyone is ready to sign.

Then the financials come out.

Personal expenses run through the business. Cash sales that never got recorded. Numbers that don't match the tax returns.

And the buyer starts to wonder what else is hidden.

That doubt is what kills the deal. Not the actual numbers. The trust.

Had a seller last year with a great location and strong sales. But his books were a mess. The buyer walked, and the next offer came in lower because word had already spread.

Clean books do two things. They make your business easier to sell, and they make it worth more.

Here's the takeaway. Start cleaning up your financials at least two years before you plan to sell. Not two weeks.

The best time to prepare for an exit is long before you need one.
